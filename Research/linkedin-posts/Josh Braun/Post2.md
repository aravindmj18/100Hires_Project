I’ve tried a lot of ways to open a cold call.

This is one that worked for me and thousands of sellers. 

Doesn’t mean it’ll work for you.

Everything I share is an experiment.

Run it.
See what happens.
Find your own way.

Hello? 
Josh? 
Yes. 
Hi, I’m Ben. We’ve never spoken, but I was hoping you could help me for a moment. 
Sure. 
Do you still handle scheduling for your plumbers when people call in? 
I do. 
Bit of a random question…

When calls come in after hours, are you using an answering service, automated text messages saying you’ll follow up in the morning, or an AI dispatcher that takes the call and books the appointment?

The psychology: 

“We’ve never spoken” lowers the guard.

“Could you help me for a moment?” gives them control.

“Do you still handle scheduling…” confirms you’re talking to the right person using their language, not sales language.

Then the A / B / C question gets them thinking about how they currently solve the problem.

A - current way
B - current way 
C - what’s possible 

No pitch.
No explanation.

Just a question that makes the status quo visible.

Don’t start with what you sell.
Start with how they currently get the job done.
