The C-suite looks at the SDR team and sees a pipeline machine. However, if it's the only way you are looking at it my belief is that it is a big mistake.

Because you're missing the most valuable thing sitting right in front of you.

A talent pipeline.

Think about this... 

Every great AE, every strong sales manager, every VP of Sales who actually knows how to build... most of them started as SDRs. 

The SDR role is where you learn:

• How to handle rejection 
• Understand human nature 
• Think on your feet
• Communicate under stress
• Build something from nothing
 
All those skills are future leaders skills to me...

Ok sure yeah you want to leverage AI in the sales org. 

Great... Who exactly is going to be inputting the knowledge into the AI? Who understands the buyer well enough to train the prompts? Who knows what a great cold call sounds like well enough to build a framework around it?

You might want to develop people who actually understand the craft before handing them technology to scale it.

This is exactly why I built Sales Team Six the way I did. 45 days developing the whole person.. not just the skill set. Because the reps who understand the craft become the leaders who scale it.

Yet most companies treat the SDR team like a secondhand option. 

You're burning the talent pipeline AND wondering why your AI strategy doesn't work..

When I managed 13 SDRs that team never missed their number... But the thing I'm most proud of isn't the pipeline we built...

Reps from that room got promoted to AEs... Some are leaders running their own teams now.

If you develop SDRs as whole people... teach them how to think not just what to say... two things happen.

They produce better pipeline right now.
AND they become the leaders you won't have to hire from the outside later.
Sales development isn't just a function.

It's the farm system for your entire revenue organization.

Treat it like one.
